## Client Overview

Client: Kingston Beta

Industry: Tech community and events

Goal: Bring tech-based persons together through events and gatherings.

Emotional anchor: Community, energy, belonging.

Brand colors: Lime green, deep forest green, red accent.

Design taste: Sleek, modern, cinematic.

Kingston Beta should be understood as a cultural tech community brand, not a conventional software company or event listing template. The experience should carry the feeling of a modern Caribbean tech movement: connected, confident, warm, and forward-moving.

## Brand Identity Reading

The Kingston Beta logo communicates:

- Forward motion through the chevron mark.
- Vibrancy through the lime green.
- Precision through the clean sans-serif typography.
- Energy through the red dot accent.
- Grounded community presence through the deep green `BETA` block.

This is a brand about:

- Forward momentum.
- Community gathering.
- Youthful tech energy.
- Bold optimism.
- Caribbean and Kingston cultural confidence.

The website should not feel like:

- A corporate tech firm.
- A SaaS landing page.
- A meetup template.
- A generic startup site.

It should feel like:

- A cultural tech movement.
- A curated community brand.
- A cinematic event platform.
- A confident modern collective.

Take forward:

- Floating brand-color dots.
- Organic asymmetrical composition.

## Strategic Positioning

Kingston Beta becomes:

A cinematic, community-led tech culture brand built around belonging, momentum, and modern Caribbean tech identity.

The website should feel like:

- A cultural movement.
- An editorial community platform.
- A curated event ecosystem.
- A sleek modern tech collective.

It should not feel:

- Corporate.
- Generic.
- Templated.
- Conference-software-like.

Strategic design principle:

Kingston Beta should frame technology as a living community experience. The brand should not center abstract product language, dashboards, or generic startup momentum. It should center people, gatherings, atmosphere, cultural confidence, and the feeling that Kingston's tech future is being built in public, together.

## Future Implementation Guardrails

- Do not redesign from scratch unless explicitly instructed.
- Preserve the reference-led diagonal composition language.
- Keep people and community imagery central.
- Use brand color dots as atmospheric accents, not decorative clutter.
- Let typography overlap, frame, and participate in the composition.
- Use motion later as spatial atmosphere, not as attention-seeking interface behavior.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences in future animation work.
- Keep the red accent controlled and intentional.
- Avoid generic card-heavy sections unless a later content need clearly requires them.
